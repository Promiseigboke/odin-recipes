Egusi Soup

Egusi is a west African soup which is rich in fat, protein, and essential nutrients. it is a traditional soup usually cooked in one-pot, and combines blended melon seed, pepper, leafy vegetables, and meat. it is particularly common in Nigeria because of it's delicious match with pounded yam.The main ingredient is the melon seed, which is derived from the melon plant in the same family as squash and gourds.

Ingredients For Making Egusi Soup:

  • Egusi melon: This is the main ingradient needed to make this soup. You can get the seed or processed one at local grocery store or African markets.
  • Palm Oil: This oil is used to cook the pepper mix. The pepper mix which is the base of the soup before adding the egusi.
  • Beef, dried fish, stock fish and Crayfish: These are my protein sources for a hearty soup. There are still a lot more out there.
  • Locust beans: This ingredient adds an earthly umami tast to the soup. You can still use seasoning cubes if you dont like locust beans.
  • Spinach: There are other leafy green vegetables like bitter leaves, uziza leaf, fluted pumpkin leaves, water leaf, and so on.
  • Pepper mix: my blended pepper mix is a combination of habanero pepper, red bell, fresno pepper, and onions.
  • Onions: You still need to define the soup's aroma in addition to the onion in the in the pepper mix.
  • Stock Cube: This is a do without but a worthwhile seasoning addition.
  • Salt: This is very important for taste.
  • Beef stock: This will enhance the flavor. You can still use chicken stockas well.

Guidelines for cooking Egusi soup

  • Blend the onions and pepper together : Blend the pepper and onions using your blender. Make sure to add little water.
  • Blend the Egusi seeds: The next thing to do is to blend the Egusi seeds, crayfish and the onions together. You can a little bit of water to give the Egusi a thick consistency.
  • Heat the palm oil but avoid overheating: Heat the oil be careful not to overheat it.
  • Add locust beans and onions: You can add seasoning cubes if you prefer that.
  • Time to add the blended pepper: Fry the Egusi for about 15 to 20 minutes, before adding the Egusi melon. Don not stir the Egusi now ; give it some time to curdle. Stir it for a while after it's curdled, then add your meat /fish of your choice with your chicken or beef and leave to cook for about 15 minutes. Now the Egusi should be cooked already, if you feel there is a need to cook a little longer just add some more water or stock and cook for a few more minutes.
  • Add the locust bean, beef stock, and salt to taste. Cover the pot of soup and leave it for 5 minutes.
  • Introduce the blended egusi inside the sauce but dont stir at this point. Cover and leave it for to cook for about 20 to 30 minutes. Keep an eye on it but do not stir until the egusi turns into a soft compact mass. You can also add more water when stiring.
  • Put the spinach and leave to cook for about 5 minutes. Your now have to stir constantly this time.
  • Serve while it's still hot.
