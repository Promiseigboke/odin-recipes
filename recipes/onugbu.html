Onugbu Soup(Bitter Leaf Soup)

Onugbu soup(ofe onugbu) which is also known as bitter leaf soup, is a traditional soup popularly enjoyed by a lot of tribes in Nigeria.

Onugbu soup is commonly eaten with fufu. Because of the presence of bitterleaf in the soup, the soup has slightly bitter taste.

This soup has a lot of nutritional and health benefits, which makes Nigerians not to joke with it.

Bitter leaf, is scientifically known as Veronia amygadalina. in Nigeria it is called Onugbu by the igbo's,Ewuro by the Yorubas, and Shuwaka by the Hausa tribe.

Ingredients Used

  • Bitter Leaf: Fresh is prefarable.
  • Meat/fish: Beef is prefarable(Goat meat). Any other animal protein is permitted.
  • Vegetables:pepper, onions, and tomatoes.
  • Broth: You can use water if you don't have broth.
  • Palm oil:By now you must have noticed that most Nigerian soupus are made with palm oil.
  • Spice:Salt, crayfish, cayenne pepper, bouillon powder.

How to cook Onugbu Soup

  1. Put the oil in a pot and add some onions. leave it for a minute.
  2. Pour in your pepper and tomatoes(blended). Add the cooked beef, smoked fish, spices, and simmer for some minutes.
  3. Add your bitter leaf and stir together.
  4. Cook on medium heat till the bitter leaf is wilted.
  5. Serve and enjoy!
